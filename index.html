A little about me.

Currently located in Nashville, Tennessee, I am a peer at Nashville Software School in Cohort 25. I graduated from the University of Tennessee in 2012 with a degree in American Studies. However, my journey to web development started close to three years ago, when I was in my role at ServiceSource as an Insides Sales Representative. There I gained valuable exposure to Javascript developers and SalesForce.com programmers. Their work was always some of the most interesting that came across my desk. I finally asked myself, “Is this the type of work I want for myself?” This led to my first Google search of what does it take to be a web developer?

Most people who are curious about programming quickly find the same underlying theme: if you enjoy problem solving, this is the career path for you. I never thought of myself as someone who loves puzzles and problem solving, but I soon found my stride in my first course by Skillscrush in Web Development. It’s where I learned that problem solving doesn’t always mean having an answer, it’s more about the willingness to try and find a solution; to not be afraid of failing. It’s about knowing how to fail better each time.
